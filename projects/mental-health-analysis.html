Mental Health Analysis

Identifying Key Factors Affecting Mental Wellbeing in the Workplace

Healthcare Analytics Machine Learning Cluster Analysis Predictive Modeling

Executive Summary

This project analyzes workplace mental health data to identify key factors affecting employee wellbeing and develops a predictive model that can accurately forecast which employees may be at risk of developing mental health conditions.

Key Achievement

Developed a Random Forest classification model that predicts mental health risks with 94.2% precision and 89.7% recall, identifying work environment and support systems as the primary factors affecting mental wellbeing.

Key Findings

Work Environment Impact

The analysis revealed a strong correlation between workplace environment and mental health:

  • 78% of employees reporting high stress worked in environments with poor work-life balance
  • Nearly 65% of employees with anxiety reported excessive workloads
  • Mental wellbeing scores drop significantly in environments with limited autonomy
  • Employees working more than 50 hours weekly showed a 3x higher risk of developing depression

Relationship between workload and mental wellbeing scores

Support Systems and Resources

The analysis found that companies with robust mental health support systems had 42% lower rates of reported mental health issues. This indicates that accessible resources and clear communication about available support significantly impact employee wellbeing.

Impact of workplace support systems on mental health outcomes

Stigma and Disclosure

Employees in environments with high perceived stigma around mental health were 67% less likely to seek help when needed. This suggests that creating a culture of openness and acceptance is crucial for early intervention and prevention.

Methodology Overview

This project followed a structured analytical approach to develop a reliable predictive model:

  1. Data Collection - Gathered comprehensive employee data including stress levels, work environment factors, support system access, and mental health outcomes
  2. Exploratory Analysis - Identified patterns and relationships between workplace factors and mental health
  3. Feature Engineering - Created new features like "high stress environment" based on multiple indicators
  4. Model Development - Tested multiple classification algorithms including Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and Logistic Regression
  5. Model Evaluation - Assessed performance using precision, recall, and F1-score metrics
  6. Feature Importance Analysis - Identified the most influential factors in predicting mental health risks

Performance comparison of different classification models

Model Selection Process

Multiple classification models were evaluated for this project:

  • Random Forest - Best overall performance with 94.2% precision and 89.7% recall
  • Gradient Boosting - Good performance but slightly lower recall (87.3%)
  • Logistic Regression - Simplest model but lowest performance (82.1% precision)

Feature Engineering

Several engineered features improved model performance:

  • Work-Life Balance Score - Composite metric based on working hours, flexibility, and time off
  • Support Access Index - Calculated from availability and quality of mental health resources
  • Stigma Perception - Derived from survey responses about workplace attitudes

Feature Importance

The Random Forest model identified these features as most predictive (in order of importance):

  1. Work-life balance score (24.8%)
  2. Weekly working hours (18.7%)
  3. Support access index (16.2%)
  4. Stigma perception (14.5%)
  5. Job autonomy (11.3%)
  6. Management quality (8.4%)
  7. Team dynamics (6.1%)

Business Applications

This predictive model offers valuable applications for HR and management teams:

Proactive Intervention

Identify at-risk employees before mental health issues develop, allowing for early support and prevention strategies.

Workplace Optimization

Design work environments and policies that promote mental wellbeing based on identified key factors.

Resource Allocation

Strategically invest in mental health resources that address the most impactful factors for employee wellbeing.

Strategic Insight

The model suggests that addressing work environment factors and reducing stigma would be more effective for improving mental health outcomes than simply adding more resources without addressing underlying issues. Organizations should prioritize creating psychologically safe environments where employees feel comfortable seeking help.

Conclusion & Future Work

This analysis provides valuable insights into the factors affecting workplace mental health and offers a reliable predictive model for identifying at-risk employees. By focusing on work-life balance, support systems, and reducing stigma, organizations can significantly improve mental health outcomes.

Future Directions

This research could be extended in several promising directions:

  • Incorporate longitudinal data to track mental health trends over time
  • Develop industry-specific models to account for different workplace stressors
  • Create an interactive dashboard for HR teams to monitor mental health risk factors
  • Expand the analysis to include additional factors like remote work impact, physical workspace design, and leadership styles
